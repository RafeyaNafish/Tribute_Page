Mark Zuckerberg Biography:

Early Life and Education:

Mark Elliot Zuckerberg was born on May 14, 1984, in White Plains, New York, USA.
He grew up in Dobbs Ferry, New York, in a well-educated and supportive family.
His father, Edward Zuckerberg, was a dentist, and his mother, Karen Zuckerberg, was a psychiatrist.
He developed an interest in programming at an early age, creating simple programs with the encouragement of his parents.
Zuckerberg attended Phillips Exeter Academy, a prestigious preparatory school, where he excelled in math, science, and classical studies.
He also developed a music recommendation software called Synapse while in high school.
Later, he enrolled at Harvard University in 2002, where he studied computer science and psychology.

In 2004, he co-founded "The Facebook" with his roommates Dustin Moskovitz, Eduardo Saverin, Chris Hughes, and Andrew McCollum.
The platform started as a social networking site for Harvard students but quickly expanded to
other Ivy League schools and eventually to universities worldwide.
Later in 2004, Zuckerberg dropped out of Harvard to focus entirely on growing Facebook.
He moved to Palo Alto, California, where he established Facebook's headquarters.

Facebook's Growth and Success:

In 2005, Facebook secured its first major investment of $12.7 million from Accel Partners.
In 2006, Facebook introduced the News Feed, which became a defining feature of the platform.
In 2007, Microsoft invested $240 million, valuing Facebook at $15 billion.

In 2021, Facebook was rebranded as Meta Platforms, reflecting a shift toward the metaverse and virtual reality.

Personal Life:

Mark Zuckerberg married Priscilla Chan in 2012. They met while studying at Harvard.
The couple has three daughters: Maxima (Max), August, and Aurelia.
Zuckerberg and Chan run the Chan Zuckerberg Initiative (CZI), a philanthropic
organization focusing on education, health, and scientific research.

Net Worth and Influence:

As of 2024, Zuckerberg is one of the world's richest people, with a net worth exceeding $100 billion.
He has been recognized as a leading figure in technology, but Facebook and Meta have faced
criticism regarding privacy issues, misinformation, and monopolistic practices.

Conclusion:

Mark Zuckerberg remains a pivotal figure in social media and technology,
continually pushing the boundaries of online communication and digital innovation.
His vision for the metaverse signals his ambition to shape the future of the internet.
